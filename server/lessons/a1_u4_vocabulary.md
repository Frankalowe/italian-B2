## 3. Possessive Adjectives (Aggettivi Possessivi)

These words show ownership and must agree in gender and number with the *noun* they modify (e.g., *mio* for masculine singular, *mia* for feminine singular).

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence (Italian) | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **mio** | my (masc. singular) | MEE-oh | Questo è mio. | This is mine. |
| **mia** | my (fem. singular) | MEE-ah | Questa è mia. | This is mine. |
| **tuo** | your (masc. singular) | TOO-oh | È tuo libro? | Is it your book? |
| **tua** | your (fem. singular) | TOO-ah | È tua casa? | Is it your house? |
| **suo** | his / her / its (formal) | SOO-oh | È suo cane. | It is his/hers dog. |
| **nostro** | our (masc. plural) | NOHS-troh | Questo è nostro. | This is ours. |
| **vostro** | your (pl. formal) | VOS-troh | È vostro tempo. | It is your time. |
| **loro** | their (plural) | LOH-roh | Sono loro amici. | They are their friends. |

## 4. Demonstratives (Aggettivi Dimostrativi)

These words point out specific things. They must agree in gender and number with the noun they modify.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence (Italian) | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **questo** | this (masc. singular) | KWEH-stoh | Questo è un libro. | This is a book. |
| **questa** | this (fem. singular) | KWEH-stah | Questa è una penna. | This is a pen. |
| **questi** | these (masc. plural) | KWEH-stee | Questi sono amici. | These are friends. |
| **queste** | these (fem. plural) | KWEH-stah | Queste sono mie. | These are mine. |
| **quello** | that (masc. singular, far from speaker) | KEHL-loh | Quello è grande. | That is big. |
| **quella** | that (fem. singular, far from speaker) | KWEHL-lah | Quella è bella. | That is beautiful. |
| **quelli** | those (masc. plural) | KWEHL-lee | Quelli sono miei. | Those are mine. |
| **quelle** | those (fem. plural) | KWEHL-leh | Quelle sono tue. | Those are yours. |

***

### 💡 Professor's Note for Practice:

**Focus on Agreement!** Notice how the ending of the adjective changes based on the noun:

*   If you are talking about **masculine singular** (e.g., *il cane*), you use the masculine ending: *alto* (Il cane è **alto**).
*   If you are talking about **feminine singular** (e.g., *la gatta*), you add an 'a': *alta* (La gatta è **alta**).

This agreement is the foundation of Italian grammar. Practice saying these sentences aloud until the pattern becomes automatic! *Buon lavoro!* (Good work!)

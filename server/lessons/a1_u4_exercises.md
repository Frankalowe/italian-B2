## 🎓 Professor's Explanations & Review

### Grammar Deep Dive: Possessives (Possessive Adjectives)

Possessive adjectives must agree in **gender** (masculine/feminine) and **number** (singular/plural) with the **thing possessed** (the noun) and **not** with the possessor (the person).

| Possessor (Who owns it?) | Singular (Masculine) | Singular (Feminine) | Plural (Masculine) | Plural (Feminine) |
| :----------------------- | :-------------------- | :------------------- | :------------------ | :----------------- |
| **Io** (I)               | mio                   | mia                  | miei                | mie                |
| **Tu** (You, singular informal) | tuo                   | tua                  | tuoi                | tue                |
| **Lui/Lei** (He/She)     | suo                   | sua                  | suoi                | sue                |
| **Noi** (We)             | nostro                | nostra               | nostri               | nostre             |
| **Voi** (You, plural)    | vostri                | vostre               | vostri               | vostre             |
| **Loro** (They)          | loro                 | loro                | loro                | loro              |

**Key Takeaway:** Notice how *mio* (masculine singular) changes to *mia* (feminine singular) when referring to a feminine noun (e.g., *mia casa*).

### Grammar Deep Dive: Demonstratives (Demonstrative Adjectives)

Demonstratives point things out. They must agree with the noun they modify in **gender** and **number**.

*   **Questo** (This): Used for singular, masculine nouns (e.g., *questo libro*).
*   **Questa** (This): Used for singular, feminine nouns (e.g., *questa casa*).
*   **Quello** (That): Used for singular, masculine nouns (e.g., *quello cane*).
*   **Quella** (That): Used for singular, feminine nouns (e.g., *quella casa*).
*   **Questi** (These): Used for plural, masculine nouns (e.g., *questi libri*).
*   **Queste** (These): Used for plural, feminine nouns (e.g., *queste case*).

### Vocabulary Review

*   **Family:** *madre* (mother), *padre* (father), *sorella* (sister), *fratello* (brother), *famiglia* (family).
*   **Descriptions:** *alto* (tall), *basso* (short), *giovane* (young), *simpatico/a* (nice/nice-looking).
*   **Pets:** *cane* (dog), *gatto* (cat).

# Advanced Subjunctive (Imperfetto & Trapassato)

## Introduction to the Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive mood (congiuntivo) in Italian expresses doubt, desire, emotion, or uncertainty. It is often used in dependent clauses that follow certain verbs, adjectives, or conjunctions. This chapter focuses on the **Congiuntivo Imperfetto** and **Congiuntivo Trapassato**, which are used to express hypothetical situations, wishes, or emotions about the past.

## 1. Congiuntivo Imperfetto

### Formation

The Congiuntivo Imperfetto is formed by taking the third person plural of the **indicative imperfect** tense, dropping the ending, and adding the subjunctive endings.

#### Regular Verb Conjugation

| Verb Type | Infinitive Ending | Conjugation Pattern | Example Verb: **parlare** (to speak) |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|---------------------------------------|
| -are      | -are              | -assi, -assi, -asse, -assimo, -aste, -assero | parlassi, parlassi, parlasse, parlassimo, parlaste, parlassero |
| -ere      | -ere              | -essi, -essi, -esse, -essimo, -este, -essero | dicessi, dicessi, dicesse, dicessimo, diceste, dicessero |
| -ire      | -ire              | -issi, -issi, -isse, -issimo, -iste, -issero | finissi, finissi, finisse, finissimo, finiste, finissero |

#### Irregular Verbs

Some verbs have irregular forms in the Congiuntivo Imperfetto. Here are a few examples:

| Verb      | Congiuntivo Imperfetto |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| essere    | fossi, fossi, fosse, fossimo, foste, fossero |
| avere     | avessi, avessi, avesse, avessimo, aveste, avessero |
| andare    | andassi, andassi, andasse, andassimo, andaste, andassero |
| fare      | facessi, facessi, facesse, facessimo, faceste, facessero |
| sapere    | sapessi, sapessi, sapesse, sapessimo, sapeste, sapessero |

### Usage

The Congiuntivo Imperfetto is typically used in the following contexts:

1. **After Verbs of Emotion or Opinion**: 
   - Example: **Speravo che tu venissi alla festa.**  
     (I hoped that you would come to the party.)

2. **In Conditional Clauses**: 
   - Example: **Se avessi tempo, andrei al cinema.**  
     (If I had time, I would go to the cinema.)

3. **In Hypothetical Situations**: 
   - Example: **Se fossi ricco, viaggerei per il mondo.**  
     (If I were rich, I would travel the world.)

## 2. Congiuntivo Trapassato

### Formation

The Congiuntivo Trapassato is formed using the imperfect subjunctive of **essere** or **avere** plus the past participle of the main verb.

#### Conjugation Structure

| Auxiliary Verb | Conjugation | Example Verb: **parlare** (to speak) |
|----------------|-------------|---------------------------------------|
| Essere         | fossi + participle | fossi stato (I had been) |
| Avere          | avessi + participle | avessi parlato (I had spoken) |

### Usage

The Congiuntivo Trapassato is used in the following contexts:

1. **To Express Doubt or Uncertainty About Past Events**:
   - Example: **Non credevo che tu avessi già finito il lavoro.**  
     (I didn't believe that you had already finished the work.)

2. **In Conditional Clauses with Past Reference**:
   - Example: **Se avessi saputo, sarei venuto.**  
     (If I had known, I would have come.)

3. **After Certain Conjunctions**:
   - Example: **Era importante che lui fosse arrivato in tempo.**  
     (It was important that he had arrived on time.)

## 3. Sequence of Tenses (Consecutio Temporum)

When using the subjunctive in the past, it is crucial to maintain the correct sequence of tenses. The general rule is:

- If the main verb is in the past (indicative), the subjunctive verb must also be in the past (imperfect or trapassato).

### Examples

1. **Main Verb in Past Indicative**:
   - **Pensavo che tu avessi ragione.**  
     (I thought that you were right.)

2. **Main Verb in Present Indicative**:
   - **Penso che tu abbia ragione.**  
     (I think that you are right.)

## 4. Example Sentences

Here are some varied example sentences demonstrating the use of the Congiuntivo Imperfetto and Trapassato:

1. **Speravo che tu venissi con noi.**  
   (I hoped that you would come with us.)

2. **Se avessi studiato di più, avresti passato l'esame.**  
   (If you had studied more, you would have passed the exam.)

3. **Era necessario che noi partissimo prima.**  
   (It was necessary for us to leave earlier.)

4. **Non credevo che loro avessero già visto quel film.**  
   (I didn't believe that they had already seen that movie.)

5. **Se fossi stato in te, avrei accettato l'offerta.**  
   (If I had been you, I would have accepted the offer.)

6. **Era strano che lui non fosse venuto alla riunione.**  
   (It was strange that he had not come to the meeting.)

7. **Se avessi avuto più tempo, avrei potuto aiutarti.**  
   (If I had had more time, I could have helped you.)

8. **Pensavo che tu avessi già parlato con il professore.**  
   (I thought that you had already spoken with the professor.)

# Unit: The Passive Voice - Grammar Guide

## Introduction to the Passive Voice

The passive voice is a grammatical construction where the subject of the sentence is acted upon by the verb. In Italian, the passive voice can be formed using the auxiliary verbs **"essere"** (to be) and **"venire"** (to come). Understanding how to form passive sentences is crucial for expressing actions without focusing on the doer.

### 1. Forming Passive Sentences

#### 1.1 Using "Essere"

The passive voice is primarily formed with the verb **"essere"** followed by the past participle of the main verb. The past participle must agree in gender and number with the subject of the sentence.

**Structure:**
\[ \text{Subject} + \text{essere (conjugated)} + \text{past participle} \]

**Example:**
- **Italian:** La lettera è scritta da Maria.
- **Literal Translation:** The letter is written by Maria.
- **Idiomatic Translation:** Maria writes the letter.

**Conjugation of "Essere":**

| Person         | Present Tense | Past Tense (Passato Prossimo) | Future Tense |
|----------------|---------------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| Io             | sono          | sono stato/a                  | sarò          |
| Tu             | sei           | sei stato/a                   | sarai         |
| Lui/Lei/Esso  | è             | è stato/a                     | sarà          |
| Noi            | siamo         | siamo stati/e                 | saremo        |
| Voi            | siete         | siete stati/e                 | sarete        |
| Loro           | sono          | sono stati/e                  | saranno       |

#### 1.2 Using "Venire"

The verb **"venire"** can also be used to form the passive voice, often to emphasize the action or the process rather than the result.

**Structure:**
\[ \text{Subject} + \text{venire (conjugated)} + \text{past participle} \]

**Example:**
- **Italian:** Il libro viene letto da molti studenti.
- **Literal Translation:** The book is come read by many students.
- **Idiomatic Translation:** Many students read the book.

**Conjugation of "Venire":**

| Person         | Present Tense | Past Tense (Passato Prossimo) | Future Tense |
|----------------|---------------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| Io             | vengo         | sono venuto/a                 | verrò         |
| Tu             | vieni         | sei venuto/a                  | verrai        |
| Lui/Lei/Esso  | viene         | è venuto/a                    | verrà         |
| Noi            | veniamo       | siamo venuti/e                | verremo       |
| Voi            | venite        | siete venuti/e                | verrete       |
| Loro           | vengono       | sono venuti/e                 | verranno      |

### 2. Expressing Necessity with "Andare" + Past Participle

In Italian, to express necessity or obligation, the construction **"andare" + past participle** is commonly used. This structure indicates that something needs to be done.

**Structure:**
\[ \text{Subject} + \text{andare (conjugated)} + \text{past participle} \]

**Example:**
- **Italian:** La casa va pulita.
- **Literal Translation:** The house goes cleaned.
- **Idiomatic Translation:** The house needs to be cleaned.

**Conjugation of "Andare":**

| Person         | Present Tense | Past Tense (Passato Prossimo) | Future Tense |
|----------------|---------------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| Io             | vado          | sono andato/a                 | andrò        |
| Tu             | vai           | sei andato/a                  | andrai       |
| Lui/Lei/Esso  | va            | è andato/a                    | andrà        |
| Noi            | andiamo       | siamo andati/e                | andremo      |
| Voi            | andate        | siete andati/e                | andrete      |
| Loro           | vanno         | sono andati/e                 | andranno     |

### 3. Agreement of the Past Participle

When using **"essere"** or **"venire"** in the passive voice, the past participle must agree in gender and number with the subject. 

- **Masculine Singular:** -o (e.g., "scritto")
- **Feminine Singular:** -a (e.g., "scritta")
- **Masculine Plural:** -i (e.g., "scritti")
- **Feminine Plural:** -e (e.g., "scritte")

### 4. Example Sentences

1. **Italian:** I progetti sono approvati dal governo.
   - **Literal Translation:** The projects are approved by the government.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** The government approves the projects.

2. **Italian:** Le leggi vengono discusse in Parlamento.
   - **Literal Translation:** The laws are come discussed in Parliament.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** The laws are discussed in Parliament.

3. **Italian:** La plastica va riciclata.
   - **Literal Translation:** The plastic goes recycled.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** The plastic needs to be recycled.

4. **Italian:** Gli alberi vengono piantati per combattere il cambiamento climatico.
   - **Literal Translation:** The trees are come planted to fight climate change.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** Trees are planted to fight climate change.

5. **Italian:** Le risorse naturali devono essere protette.
   - **Literal Translation:** The natural resources must be protected.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** Natural resources must be protected.

6. **Italian:** La qualità dell'aria è migliorata grazie alle nuove politiche.
   - **Literal Translation:** The quality of the air is improved thanks to the new policies.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** Air quality has improved thanks to new policies.

7. **Italian:** I rifiuti vanno smaltiti in modo corretto.
   - **Literal Translation:** The waste goes disposed of in a correct way.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** Waste needs to be disposed of correctly.

8. **Italian:** Le emissioni di CO2 sono ridotte ogni anno.
   - **Literal Translation:** The emissions of CO2 are reduced every year.
   - **Idiomatic Translation:** CO2 emissions are reduced every year.

### Conclusion

Understanding the passive voice in Italian is essential for effective communication, especially in contexts related to environmental policies and discussions on climate change. Mastery of the structures involving **"essere," "venire,"** and **"andare"** will enhance your ability to express actions and necessities clearly and accurately.

**Unit 1: Adjectives, Possessives & Demonstratives**

**Grammar Guide**

### Adjectives Agreement

In Italian, adjectives must agree with the noun they modify in terms of gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural). This agreement is crucial for correct sentence structure.

*   **Masculine singular**: ends in -o (e.g., bello "beautiful")
*   **Feminine singular**: ends in -a (e.g., bella "beautiful")
*   **Masculine plural**: ends in -i (e.g., belli "beautiful")
*   **Feminine plural**: ends in -e (e.g., belle "beautiful")

### Possessive Adjectives

Possessive adjectives indicate ownership or possession. They agree with the noun they modify.

| Possessive Adjective | English Translation |
| --- | --- |
| mio | my |
| tuo | your |
| suo | his/her/its |
| nostro | our |
| vostro | your (formal) |
| loro | their |

### Demonstratives

Demonstrative adjectives indicate which noun is being referred to.

*   **Questo** (masculine singular): this
*   **Quella** (feminine singular): that
*   **Questi** (masculine plural): these
*   **Quelle** (feminine plural): those

### Example Sentences

1.  Il mio amico è molto simpatico. (My friend is very nice.)
2.  Questa casa è bella, ma quella è più grande. (This house is beautiful, but that one is bigger.)
3.  Io ho un cane giovane e lui è molto alto. (I have a young dog and he is very tall.)
4.  La mia sorella è molto intelligente e lavora come ingegnere. (My sister is very intelligent and works as an engineer.)
5.  Il mio fratello è simpatico, ma il tuo non lo è. (My brother is nice, but yours isn't.)

### Common Mistakes & Systematic Errors

1.  **False Friends**: English speakers often confuse words like "bella" (beautiful) with "bell" (ring), leading to incorrect usage.
2.  **Pronoun Placement**: Incorrect placement of pronouns can change the meaning of a sentence, e.g., "Il mio amico è molto simpatico" vs. "Mio amico è molto simpatico".
3.  **Wrong Auxiliary Verbs**: Using the wrong auxiliary verb (e.g., "avere" instead of "essere") can lead to incorrect tense usage.
4.  **Lack of Adjective Agreement**: Failing to agree adjectives with nouns can result in grammatically incorrect sentences.

### Practical Dialogue Scenario

**Dialogue:**

1.  A: Ciao, come stai?
2.  B: Sto bene, grazie. E tu?
3.  A: Sono un po' stanco.
4.  B: Ah, capisco. Vieni a casa mia e riposati.
5.  A: Grazie, ma non posso. Devo andare da mio fratello.
6.  B: Va bene, allora vado anch'io.

**English Translation:**

1.  A: Hi, how are you?
2.  B: I'm fine, thanks. And you?
3.  A: I'm a bit tired.
4.  B: Ah, I understand. Come to my house and rest.
5.  A: Thanks, but I can't. I have to go see my brother.
6.  B: Okay, then I'll come too.

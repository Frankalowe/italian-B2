# Masterclass Italian: A1 Vocabulary & Expressions - Unit 2: Articles, Gender & Number

Welcome to the vocabulary enrichment segment for our unit on "Articles, Gender & Number." This section is designed to equip you with essential Italian words and phrases that will allow you to practice and solidify your understanding of definite and indefinite articles, noun gender and number, and basic noun-adjective agreement.

Each entry includes the Italian word/phrase, its English meaning, a phonetic pronunciation guide tailored for English speakers, a contextual example sentence in Italian, and its English translation. Pay close attention to how articles change with gender and number, and how adjectives adapt to the nouns they describe.

## I. Definite Articles (Articoli Determinativi) - Reference

These are the Italian equivalents of "the." Their form changes based on the gender, number, and initial letter of the noun they precede.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence in Italian | English Translation |
| :-------------------- | :-------------- | :--------------------------- | :------------------------------------- | :------------------ |
| **il**                | the (masc. sing.) | *eel*                        | **Il** libro è rosso.                  | The book is red.    |
| **lo**                | the (masc. sing. before s+consonant, z, gn, ps, x, y) | *loh*                        | **Lo** studente è qui.                 | The student is here. |
| **l'** (masc. sing.)  | the (masc. sing. before vowel) | *l'* (like 'l' in 'let')     | **L'**albero è alto.                   | The tree is tall.   |
| **la**                | the (fem. sing.) | *lah*                        | **La** penna è blu.                    | The pen is blue.    |
| **l'** (fem. sing.)   | the (fem. sing. before vowel) | *l'* (like 'l' in 'let')     | **L'**acqua è fresca.                  | The water is fresh. |
| **i**                 | the (masc. plural) | *ee*                         | **I** libri sono rossi.                | The books are red.  |
| **gli**               | the (masc. plural before vowel, s+consonant, z, etc.) | *lyee*                       | **Gli** studenti sono qui.             | The students are here. |
| **le**                | the (fem. plural) | *leh*                        | **Le** penne sono blu.                 | The pens are blue.  |

## II. Indefinite Articles (Articoli Indeterminativi) - Reference

These are the Italian equivalents of "a" or "an." Their form changes based on the gender and initial letter of the noun they precede.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence in Italian | English Translation |
| :-------------------- | :-------------- | :--------------------------- | :------------------------------------- | :------------------ |
| **un**                | a, an (masc. sing.) | *oon*                        | Ho **un** libro.                       | I have a book.      |
| **uno**               | a, an (masc. sing. before s+consonant, z, gn, ps, x, y) | *oo-noh*                     | Vedo **uno** studente.                 | I see a student.    |
| **una**               | a, an (fem. sing.) | *oo-nah*                     | Compro **una** penna.                  | I buy a pen.        |
| **un'**               | a, an (fem. sing. before vowel) | *oon'* (like 'oon' then stop) | Bevo **un'**acqua.                     | I drink some water. |

## VIII. Basic Questions & Demonstratives

These phrases help you inquire about and point to objects, reinforcing the use of articles and noun agreement.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence in Italian | English Translation |
| :-------------------- | :-------------- | :--------------------------- | :------------------------------------- | :------------------ |
| **Che cos'è?**        | What is it?     | *keh koh-ZEH*                | **Che cos'è** questo? È un libro.      | What is this? It's a book. |
| **Cos'è?**            | What is it? (shorter) | *koh-ZEH*                    | **Cos'è** quella cosa? È una penna.    | What is that thing? It's a pen. |
| **Questo/a**          | This (masc./fem. sing.) | *KWEH-stoh / KWEH-stah*      | **Questo** è il mio zaino. **Questa** è la mia borsa. | This is my backpack. This is my bag. |
| **Questi/e**          | These (masc./fem. plural) | *KWEH-stee / KWEH-steh*      | **Questi** sono i miei libri. **Queste** sono le mie chiavi. | These are my books. These are my keys. |
| **È un/una...**       | It is a...      | *eh oon / oo-nah*            | **È un** tavolo. **È una** sedia.      | It is a table. It is a chair. |
| **Sono...**           | They are...     | *SOH-noh*                    | **Sono** libri. **Sono** penne.        | They are books. They are pens. |

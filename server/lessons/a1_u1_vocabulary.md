# Chapter 1: Alphabet & Pronunciation - Vocabulary & Expressions Sheet

Welcome, aspiring linguist, to the foundational vocabulary for our "Alphabet & Pronunciation" unit. While the primary focus of this chapter is on mastering the sounds and written forms of Italian, a language is best learned in context. This curated list of essential words and phrases will not only equip you with immediate communicative tools but also serve as practical examples for applying the pronunciation rules you are simultaneously acquiring.

Pay close attention to the phonetic guides, as they are designed to bridge the gap between English and Italian sounds, highlighting stress patterns and specific Italian phonemes (like the 'ch' in *ciao* or the 'ny' in *spagnolo*). Practice these words aloud, repeatedly, to internalize both their meaning and their authentic Italian sound.

## I. Greetings & Basic Social Interactions

This section introduces fundamental phrases for initiating and concluding conversations, crucial for any beginner. Observe the nuances between formal and informal address, and how simple greetings can set the tone for interaction.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence (Italian) | English Translation of Example Sentence |
| :-------------------- | :-------------- | :--------------------------- | :----------------------------------- | :------------------------------------ |
| **Ciao**              | Hi / Hello / Bye | (CHOW)                       | Ciao, come stai?                     | Hi, how are you?                      |
| **Buongiorno**        | Good morning / Good day | (BWON-JOR-noh)               | Buongiorno, signora Rossi.           | Good morning, Mrs. Rossi.             |
| **Buonasera**         | Good evening    | (BWOH-nah-SEH-rah)           | Buonasera, professore.               | Good evening, professor.              |
| **Buonanotte**        | Good night (farewell) | (BWOH-nah-NOT-teh)           | Buonanotte, a domani!                | Good night, see you tomorrow!         |
| **Arrivederci**       | Goodbye (formal/plural) | (ah-ree-veh-DER-chee)        | Arrivederci, signori.                | Goodbye, sirs/ladies and gentlemen.   |
| **Salve**             | Hello (neutral, less informal than Ciao) | (SAL-veh)                    | Salve, posso aiutarla?               | Hello, can I help you?                |
| **Come stai?**        | How are you? (informal) | (KOH-meh STAI?)              | Ciao Marco, come stai?               | Hi Marco, how are you?                |
| **Come sta?**         | How are you? (formal) | (KOH-meh STA?)               | Buongiorno, signora, come sta?       | Good morning, madam, how are you?     |
| **Sto bene, grazie.** | I'm fine, thank you. | (STOH BEH-neh, GRAHT-see-eh) | Sto bene, grazie, e tu?              | I'm fine, thank you, and you?         |
| **Grazie (mille)!**   | Thank you (very much)! | (GRAHT-see-eh MEE-leh)       | Grazie mille per l'aiuto!            | Thank you very much for the help!     |
| **Prego.**            | You're welcome / Please. | (PREH-goh)                   | Grazie per il caffè! Prego.          | Thanks for the coffee! You're welcome. |
| **Sì.**               | Yes.            | (SEE)                        | Sei italiano? Sì.                    | Are you Italian? Yes.                 |
| **No.**               | No.             | (NOH)                        | Parli inglese? No.                   | Do you speak English? No.             |

## II. Asking & Giving Names / Spelling

This section provides the essential phrases for introducing oneself and others, and for clarifying spellings – a critical skill when dealing with unfamiliar names, especially in a new language. These phrases directly apply the alphabet knowledge you are gaining.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence (Italian) | English Translation of Example Sentence |
| :-------------------- | :-------------- | :--------------------------- | :----------------------------------- | :------------------------------------ |
| **Come ti chiami?**   | What's your name? (informal) | (KOH-meh tee KYAH-mee?)      | Ciao, come ti chiami?                | Hi, what's your name?                 |
| **Mi chiamo...**      | My name is...   | (MEE KYAH-moh...)            | Mi chiamo Sofia.                     | My name is Sofia.                     |
| **Come si chiama?**   | What's his/her name? (formal/3rd person) | (KOH-meh see KYAH-mah?)      | Come si chiama il professore?        | What's the professor's name?          |
| **Si chiama...**      | His/Her name is... | (SEE KYAH-mah...)            | Si chiama Marco.                     | His name is Marco.                    |
| **Come si scrive?**   | How do you spell it? | (KOH-meh see SKREE-veh?)     | Il tuo nome, come si scrive?         | Your name, how do you spell it?       |
| **Si scrive...**      | It's spelled... | (SEE SKREE-veh...)           | Si scrive R-O-S-S-I.                 | It's spelled R-O-S-S-I.               |
| **Lettera**           | Letter (of the alphabet) | (LET-teh-rah)                | Qual è la prima lettera del tuo nome? | What is the first letter of your name? |
| **Nome**              | Name            | (NOH-meh)                    | Il mio nome è Paolo.                 | My name is Paolo.                     |
| **Cognome**           | Last name / Surname | (koh-NYOH-meh)               | Qual è il tuo cognome?               | What is your last name?               |

## III. Nationalities

Learning nationalities allows you to describe yourself and others, and is an excellent way to practice adjective agreement (masculine/feminine, singular/plural), a core grammatical concept. Note how some nationalities have distinct masculine and feminine forms, while others remain unchanged.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence (Italian) | English Translation of Example Sentence |
| :-------------------- | :-------------- | :--------------------------- | :----------------------------------- | :------------------------------------ |
| **Italiano / Italiana** | Italian (m/f)   | (ee-tah-LYAH-noh / ee-tah-LYAH-nah) | Sono italiano. / Sono italiana.      | I am Italian (m). / I am Italian (f). |
| **Americano / Americana** | American (m/f)  | (ah-meh-ree-KAH-noh / ah-meh-ree-KAH-nah) | Lui è americano. Lei è americana.    | He is American. She is American.      |
| **Inglese**           | English (m/f)   | (een-GLEH-zeh)               | Sei inglese?                         | Are you English?                      |
| **Francese**          | French (m/f)    | (fran-CHEH-zeh)              | Parla francese?                      | Do you speak French?                  |
| **Tedesco / Tedesca** | German (m/f)    | (teh-DES-koh / teh-DES-kah)  | La mia amica è tedesca.              | My friend (f.) is German.             |
| **Spagnolo / Spagnola** | Spanish (m/f)   | (spah-NYOH-loh / spah-NYOH-lah) | Il mio professore è spagnolo.        | My professor (m.) is Spanish.         |
| **Cinese**            | Chinese (m/f)   | (chee-NEH-zeh)               | È una studentessa cinese.            | She is a Chinese student.             |
| **Giapponese**        | Japanese (m/f)  | (jahp-poh-NEH-zeh)           | Loro sono giapponesi.                | They are Japanese.                    |
| **Russo / Russa**     | Russian (m/f)   | (ROOS-soh / ROOS-sah)        | È russo o russa?                     | Is he Russian or is she Russian?      |

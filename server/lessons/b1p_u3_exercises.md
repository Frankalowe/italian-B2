# Study Notes: Exercises on Reported Speech (Discorso Indiretto)

## Overview of Reported Speech
Reported speech, or "discorso indiretto," is used to convey what someone else has said without quoting them directly. This involves changes in tense, pronouns, and sometimes time expressions. 

### Key Points:
1. **Tense Changes**: 
   - Present tense in direct speech often changes to the past tense in reported speech.
   - Past tense in direct speech typically changes to a more distant past tense in reported speech.
   - Future tense in direct speech changes to "would" or "was going to" in reported speech.

2. **Pronoun Changes**: 
   - Pronouns must be adjusted according to the perspective of the speaker.

3. **Time Expressions**: 
   - Time indicators may also change (e.g., "oggi" to "quel giorno," "domani" to "il giorno dopo").

## Exercises

### Fill-in-the-Blank Practice Sentences
Complete the following sentences by filling in the blanks with the correct form of the verb or vocabulary word.

1. Maria ha detto che _______ (essere) stanca.  
   *(Maria said that she was tired.)*

2. Il giornalista ha riportato che il presidente _______ (annunciare) nuove misure.  
   *(The journalist reported that the president announced new measures.)*

3. L'insegnante ha spiegato che gli studenti _______ (dovere) studiare di più.  
   *(The teacher explained that the students had to study more.)*

4. Paolo ha affermato che _______ (essere) importante ascoltare le notizie.  
   *(Paolo stated that it was important to listen to the news.)*

5. La stampa ha comunicato che il concerto _______ (essere) cancellato.  
   *(The press communicated that the concert was canceled.)*

### Sentence-Translation Exercises
Translate the following sentences from English to Italian.

1. She said that she would call me later.
   
2. The news reported that the weather would improve.

3. He mentioned that he had seen the movie last week.

4. They claimed that the project was successful.

5. The teacher told us that we needed to complete the assignment.

### Situational Writing Prompts
Write a short paragraph based on the following prompts.

1. You are at a café and overhear a conversation about a new restaurant opening. Write a message to a friend reporting what you heard.

2. Imagine you are a journalist covering a press conference. Write a brief report on what the speaker announced.

3. You spoke to a friend who just returned from a trip. Write a note to another friend summarizing what your friend told you about their experience.

### Multiple Choice Grammar Quiz
Choose the correct answer for each question.

1. If Maria says, "I am happy," in reported speech it becomes:
   - A) Maria ha detto che era felice.
   - B) Maria ha detto che è felice.
   - C) Maria ha detto che sarà felice.

2. When converting "I will go" to reported speech, it becomes:
   - A) Ha detto che andrà.
   - B) Ha detto che andava.
   - C) Ha detto che andò.

3. In reported speech, "I saw him yesterday" changes to:
   - A) Ha detto che lo vedeva ieri.
   - B) Ha detto che lo aveva visto il giorno prima.
   - C) Ha detto che lo vedrà ieri.

4. The phrase "We are studying" in reported speech becomes:
   - A) Ha detto che studiavano.
   - B) Ha detto che stanno studiando.
   - C) Ha detto che studieranno.

5. "I will finish the project" reported becomes:
   - A) Ha detto che finisce il progetto.
   - B) Ha detto che finiva il progetto.
   - C) Ha detto che avrebbe finito il progetto.

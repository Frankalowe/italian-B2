## Group 4: Prepositions of Place and Duration (Preposizioni di Luogo e Durata)

These are the core prepositions that govern how you describe location and time relationships.

| Italian Word / Phrase | English Meaning | Phonetic Pronunciation Guide | Contextual Example Sentence (Italian) | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **di** | of / from (possession/origin) | dee | Questo è il libro di Marco. | This is Marco's book. |
| **a** | to / at (destination/time) | ah | Vado a casa. | I go home. |
| **da** | from / since (origin/start time) | dah | Vengo da Roma. | I come from Rome. |
| **in** | in / into (location/time period) | een | Sono in cucina. | I am in the kitchen. |
| **su** | on / upon (surface) | soo | Il libro è su il tavolo. | The book is on the table. |
| **con** | with (accompaniment) | kohn | Vado con te. | I go with you. |
| **per** | for / for a duration | pair | Aspetto per due ore. | I am waiting for two hours. |
| **tra** | between (two things) | trah | La città è tra Milano e Roma. | The city is between Milan and Rome. |
| **fra** | among / among (more than two) | frah | Ci sono tre libri fra questi. | There are three books among these. |

### 💡 Professor's Note for the Learner: Mastering the Prepositions

The most challenging part for English speakers is understanding *when* to use which preposition.

1.  **Di vs. A:**
    *   Use **DI** when talking about possession or defining something (e.g., *il libro **di** Marco* – the book *of* Marco).
    *   Use **A** when talking about movement *to* a place (e.g., *vado **a** casa* – I go *to* home).

2.  **IN vs. SU:**
    *   Use **IN** for larger spaces or containers (e.g., *sono **in** Italia* – I am *in* Italy).
    *   Use **SU** for surfaces (e.g., *il libro è **su** il tavolo* – the book is *on* the table).

3.  **DA vs. PER:**
    *   Use **DA** for origin or starting point (e.g., *vengo **da** Napoli* – I come *from* Naples).
    *   Use **PER** for duration or purpose (e.g., *aspetto **per** un'ora* – I am waiting *for* one hour).

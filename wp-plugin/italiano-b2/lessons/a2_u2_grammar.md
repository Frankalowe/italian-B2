# Unit: Reflexive Verbs & Routines (Grammar Masterclass)

## Chapter 3: Riflessivi e Routine Quotidiane (Reflexive Verbs & Daily Routines)

Welcome to this chapter, where we delve into the structure of Italian verbs that describe actions performed by the subject upon themselves. Understanding reflexive verbs is essential for describing daily routines, personal care, and self-directed activities.

---

## Section 1: Introduction to Reflexive Verbs (I Verbi Riflessivi)

### 1.1 What are Reflexive Verbs?

Reflexive verbs are verbs where the action of the sentence reflects back onto the subject. When you perform an action on yourself, you use a reflexive verb.

**Structure:** Subject + Reflexive Pronoun + Verb + (Rest of the sentence)

**Examples:**
*   *Io mi lavo.* (I wash myself.)
*   *Tu ti vesti.* (You dress yourself.)
*   *Lui si sveglia.* (He wakes up himself.)

## Section 2: The Reflexive Pronouns (I Pronomi Riflessivi)

Reflexive pronouns replace the subject when the action is directed back to the subject. They must agree in gender and number with the subject.

### 2.1 Pronoun Table

| Subject Pronoun (Soggetto) | Reflexive Pronoun (Pronome Riflessivo) | English Equivalent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Io (I) | **mi** | myself |
| Tu (You, singular informal) | **ti** | yourself |
| Lui (He) | **si** | himself |
| Lei (She) | **si** | herself |
| Noi (We) | **ci** | ourselves |
| Voi (You, plural) | **vi** | yourselves |
| Loro (They) | **si** | themselves |

**Note on *Si***: The pronoun *si* is used for all third-person singular (Lui, Lei, Loro) and plural (Voi) subjects.

### 2.2 Pronoun Placement Rule (La Posizione del Pronome)

**Rule:** The reflexive pronoun **must** be placed immediately **before** the conjugated verb.

**Example:**
*   *Io mi lavo.* (I wash myself.)
*   *Loro si vestono.* (They get dressed.)

## Section 3: Conjugation of Reflexive Verbs

We will examine the conjugation in the two most important tenses: the Present Tense (for routines) and the Past Tense (for completed actions).

### 3.1 Present Tense (Presente Indicativo)

Used to describe habitual actions or current routines.

#### A. *Svegliarsi* (To wake up)

| Pronoun | Conjugation | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Io | **mi sveglio** | I wake up |
| Tu | **ti svegli** | You wake up |
| Lui/Lei/Si | **si sveglia** | He/She wakes up |
| Noi | **ci svegliamo** | We wake up |
| Voi | **vi svegliate** | You (pl.) wake up |
| Loro | **si svegliano** | They wake up |

#### B. *Vestirsi* (To get dressed)

| Pronoun | Conjugation | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Io | **mi vesto** | I get dressed |
| Tu | **ti vesti** | You get dressed |
| Lui/Lei/Si | **si veste** | He/She gets dressed |
| Noi | **ci vestiamo** | We get dressed |
| Voi | **vi vestite** | You (pl.) get dressed |
| Loro | **si vestono** | They get dressed |

### 3.2 Past Tense (Passato Prossimo)

Used to describe actions that have been completed. The reflexive pronoun is placed before the auxiliary verb (*essere* or *avere*).

**Rule:** Reflexive verbs use the auxiliary verb **essere** (to be) in the *passato prossimo*.

#### A. *Svegliarsi* (To wake up)

| Pronoun | Conjugation | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Io | **mi sono svegliato/a** | I have woken up |
| Tu | **ti sei svegliato/a** | You have woken up |
| Lui/Lei/Si | **si è svegliato/a** | He/She has woken up |
| Noi | **ci siamo svegliati/e** | We have woken up |
| Voi | **vi siete svegliati/e** | You (pl.) have woken up |
| Loro | **si sono svegliati/e** | They have woken up |

#### B. *Divertirsi* (To have fun)

| Pronoun | Conjugation | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Io | **mi sono divertito/a** | I have had fun |
| Tu | **ti sei divertito/a** | You have had fun |
| Lui/Lei/Si | **si è divertito/a** | He/She has had fun |
| Noi | **ci siamo divertiti/e** | We have had fun |
| Voi | **vi siete divertiti/e** | You (pl.) have had fun |
| Loro | **si sono divertiti/e** | They have had fun |

## Section 5: Application and Example Sentences

Here are eight comprehensive examples demonstrating the use of reflexive verbs in context, along with their translations.

**1. Present Tense (Routines)**

*   **Italian:** Io mi sveglio sempre alle sette.
    *   **Literal Translation:** I myself wake up always at seven.
    *   **Idiomatic English:** I always wake up at seven.

*   **Italian:** Tu ti vesti velocemente la mattina.
    *   **Literal Translation:** You yourself get dressed quickly the morning.
    *   **Idiomatic English:** You get dressed quickly in the morning.

*   **Italian:** Lei si lava i denti ogni giorno.
    *   **Literal Translation:** She herself washes the teeth every day.
    *   **Idiomatic English:** She washes her teeth every day.

*   **Italian:** Noi ci divertiamo molto al parco.
    *   **Literal Translation:** We ourselves have fun very in the park.
    *   **Idiomatic English:** We have a lot of fun in the park.

**2. Past Tense (Completed Actions)**

*   **Italian:** Ieri mi sono svegliato tardi.
    *   **Literal Translation:** Yesterday I myself have woken up late.
    *   **Idiomatic English:** I woke up late yesterday.

*   **Italian:** Voi vi siete divertiti alla festa?
    *   **Literal Translation:** You (pl.) yourselves have had fun at the party?
    *   **Idiomatic English:** Did you all have fun at the party?

*   **Italian:** Lui si è lavato il viso dopo la doccia.
    *   **Literal Translation:** He himself has washed the face after the shower.
    *   **Idiomatic English:** He washed his face after the shower.

*   **Italian:** Noi ci siamo preparati per la gita.
    *   **Literal Translation:** We ourselves have prepared for the trip.
    *   **Idiomatic English:** We got ready for the trip.

## Summary Checklist for Mastery

1.  **Identify the Action:** Does the action reflect back on the subject? (If yes, use a reflexive verb.)
2.  **Choose the Pronoun:** Select the correct reflexive pronoun (*mi, ti, si, ci, vi*).
3.  **Check Placement:** Place the pronoun **before** the conjugated verb.
4.  **Choose the Tense:** Use the **Presente** for routines and the **Passato Prossimo** for completed actions.
5.  **Conjugate Correctly:** Ensure the reflexive pronoun agrees with the subject, and the verb conjugation follows the rules for *essere* or *avere*.

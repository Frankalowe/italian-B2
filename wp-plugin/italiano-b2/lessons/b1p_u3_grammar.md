# Unit: Reported Speech (Discorso Indiretto)

## Introduction to Reported Speech
Reported speech, or "discorso indiretto," is used to convey what someone else has said without quoting their exact words. This form is essential in journalism, media, and everyday conversation, allowing speakers to summarize or paraphrase statements made by others.

## Key Concepts of Reported Speech

### 1. Tense Changes
When converting direct speech to reported speech, it is essential to adjust the verb tenses according to specific rules. The general principle is that the tense of the verb in the direct speech shifts back one tense in reported speech.

#### Tense Shifts Overview

| Direct Speech Tense | Reported Speech Tense |
|---------------------|-----------------------|
| Present Simple      | Past Simple           |
| Present Continuous   | Past Continuous       |
| Present Perfect     | Past Perfect          |
| Past Simple         | Past Perfect          |
| Past Continuous     | Past Perfect Continuous|
| Future Simple       | Conditional           |
| Future Perfect      | Conditional Perfect    |

### 2. Examples of Tense Changes

- **Direct Speech:** Maria dice: "Vado al mercato."  
  **Reported Speech:** Maria dice che va al mercato.  
  *(Maria says: "I am going to the market." → Maria says that she is going to the market.)*

- **Direct Speech:** Marco ha detto: "Ho visto un film."  
  **Reported Speech:** Marco ha detto che aveva visto un film.  
  *(Marco said: "I saw a movie." → Marco said that he had seen a movie.)*

- **Direct Speech:** Luca dirà: "Andrò alla festa."  
  **Reported Speech:** Luca dirà che andrà alla festa.  
  *(Luca will say: "I will go to the party." → Luca will say that he will go to the party.)*

### 3. Pronouns and Possessives
When converting to reported speech, pronouns and possessive adjectives may also need to change to reflect the perspective of the speaker.

#### Example of Pronoun Changes

- **Direct Speech:** "Io ho il libro."  
  **Reported Speech:** Lui dice che ha il libro.  
  *(He says that he has the book.)*

### 4. Time and Place Expressions
Time and place expressions often change in reported speech to reflect the shift in context.

| Direct Speech Expression | Reported Speech Expression |
|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| oggi (today)             | quel giorno (that day)     |
| domani (tomorrow)        | il giorno dopo (the next day) |
| ieri (yesterday)         | il giorno prima (the day before) |
| adesso (now)             | allora (then)              |
| qui (here)               | lì (there)                 |

### 5. Quoting Sources in Journalism
In journalism, it is crucial to attribute statements accurately. The use of reported speech allows journalists to convey information without direct quotes, maintaining the integrity of the source.

#### Example Sentences in Context

1. **Direct Speech:** "Il governo ha annunciato nuove misure."  
   **Reported Speech:** Il governo ha annunciato che ci sono nuove misure.  
   *(The government announced that there are new measures.)*

2. **Direct Speech:** "Non posso commentare l'argomento."  
   **Reported Speech:** Il portavoce ha dichiarato che non poteva commentare l'argomento.  
   *(The spokesperson stated that he could not comment on the matter.)*

3. **Direct Speech:** "Siamo soddisfatti dei risultati."  
   **Reported Speech:** I membri del team hanno affermato che erano soddisfatti dei risultati.  
   *(The team members claimed that they were satisfied with the results.)*

4. **Direct Speech:** "La conferenza si terrà domani."  
   **Reported Speech:** Hanno annunciato che la conferenza si sarebbe tenuta il giorno dopo.  
   *(They announced that the conference would be held the next day.)*

5. **Direct Speech:** "Abbiamo raggiunto un accordo."  
   **Reported Speech:** Hanno riferito che avevano raggiunto un accordo.  
   *(They reported that they had reached an agreement.)*

6. **Direct Speech:** "Il film è stato un successo."  
   **Reported Speech:** I critici hanno detto che il film era stato un successo.  
   *(The critics said that the film had been a success.)*

7. **Direct Speech:** "L'evento attirerà molti partecipanti."  
   **Reported Speech:** Gli organizzatori hanno previsto che l'evento avrebbe attirato molti partecipanti.  
   *(The organizers predicted that the event would attract many participants.)*

8. **Direct Speech:** "La situazione è migliorata."  
   **Reported Speech:** Il reporter ha riferito che la situazione era migliorata.  
   *(The reporter stated that the situation had improved.)*

## Conclusion
Understanding reported speech is crucial for effective communication in Italian, especially in contexts involving media and journalism. Mastery of tense changes, pronoun adjustments, and the correct use of time expressions will enhance your ability to convey information accurately and fluently. Practice converting direct speech to reported speech to solidify your understanding and application of these rules.

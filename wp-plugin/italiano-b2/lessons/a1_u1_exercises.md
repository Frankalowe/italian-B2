## Professor's Explanations & Review

### Key Pronunciation Rules Review

*   **Double Consonants (e.g., *caffè*, *penna*):** When you see two identical consonants (like CC, LL, SS, FF, GG), you must pronounce both sounds clearly and distinctly. This is a key difference from English, where the second consonant is often silent.
*   **Vowel Sounds:** Italian vowels are generally pure. The difference between open vowels (like *a*, *e*, *o*) and closed vowels (like *i*, *u*) is important for understanding the sound structure of words.
*   **C/G before E/I:** This is a major pronunciation rule. When 'C' or 'G' is followed by 'e' or 'i', it creates a soft, breathy sound, similar to the English 'ch' in *chair*. (e.g., *cena* sounds like 'chena').

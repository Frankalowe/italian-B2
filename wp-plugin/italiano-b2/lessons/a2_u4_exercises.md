# [ANSWERS] & Explanations

### Section 1: Fill-in-the-Blank Answers

1.  **volevo** (I wanted to stay home...)
    *   *Explanation:* *Volere* (to want) in the imperfect past tense (*volevo*) is used to express a past desire.
2.  **sapevo** (I knew how to manage...)
    *   *Explanation:* *Sapere* (to know how to/to know) in the imperfect past tense (*sapevo*) is used to express a past ability.
3.  **dovevamo** (We had to go...)
    *   *Explanation:* *Dovere* (to have to/must) in the imperfect past tense (*dovevamo*) is used to express a past obligation.
4.  **potevo** (I was able to understand...)
    *   *Explanation:* *Potere* (to be able to/can) in the imperfect past tense (*potevo*) is used to express a past ability.
5.  **voleva** (She wanted to visit...)
    *   *Explanation:* *Volere* (to want) in the imperfect past tense (*voleva*) is used to express a past desire.

### Professor's Summary Notes on Modal Past Tenses

**Key Takeaway for A2 Learners:** When talking about the past, the simple past tense (like *I wanted*) is often replaced by the **Imperfect Past** (e.g., *io volevo*). This tense is crucial for describing ongoing states, repeated actions, or past abilities/obligations.

| Modal Verb | Imperfect Past Tense (Used for Past Context) | English Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Potere** | **Potevo** | I could / I was able to | *Potevo* andare al mercato. (I could go to the market.) |
| **Volere** | **Volevo** | I wanted | *Volevo* un po' di riposo. (I wanted some rest.) |
| **Dovere** | **Dovevo** | I had to / I must | *Dovevo* studiare. (I had to study.) |
| **Sapere** | **Sapevo** | I knew how to | *Sapevo* cucinare. (I knew how to cook.) |

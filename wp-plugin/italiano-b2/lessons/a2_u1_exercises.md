## 📚 Professor's Explanations and Review

### Key Concept Review: Choosing the Auxiliary Verb (*Essere* vs. *Avere*)

The most crucial part of the *Passato Prossimo* is knowing whether to use **essere** or **avere** as the auxiliary verb.

**1. Use *AVERE*** (Most verbs)
*   Use **avere** for the vast majority of transitive verbs (verbs that take a direct object) and verbs related to possession, eating, drinking, or receiving.
    *   *Esempio:* Io **ho** mangiato (I have eaten).
    *   *Esempio:* Noi **abbiamo** visto (We have seen).
    *   *Esempio:* Io **ho** comprato (I have bought).

**2. Use *ESSERE*** (Movement and State Changes)
*   Use **essere** for verbs that describe movement, location, state of being, or change of condition. This is often called the "Mnemonic" or "Location/Movement" rule.
    *   **The Mnemonic (Location/Movement):** Use *essere* for verbs that describe **M**ovement, **E**nter, **R**eaching, **I**nteraction, **S**tate (where you are going or being).
    *   *Esempio:* Io **sono** andato (I went).
    *   *Esempio:* Tu **sei** arrivato (You arrived).
    *   *Esempio:* Noi **siamo** stati (We were).

### Agreement Rules (Crucial for *Essere*)

When you use **essere** as the auxiliary verb, the past participle **MUST** agree in gender and number with the subject.

*   **If the subject is Feminine Singular:** Add **-a** (e.g., *lei è andat**a***).
*   **If the subject is Masculine Singular:** Add **-o** (e.g., *lui è andat**o***).
*   **If the subject is Feminine Plural:** Add **-e** (e.g., *loro sono andat**e***).
*   **If the subject is Masculine Plural:** Add **-i** (e.g., *loro sono andat**i***).

**Example Review:**
*   *Loro sono andati* (Masculine Plural)
*   *Loro sono andate* (Feminine Plural)
*   *Loro sono stati* (Masculine Plural)
*   *Loro sono state* (Feminine Plural)

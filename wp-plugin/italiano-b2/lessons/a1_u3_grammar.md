# Chapter X: The Present Indicative – Essere, Avere, and Regular Verbs

## Introduction: The Foundation of Italian Verbs

In Italian, verbs are the core of any sentence, and understanding how they change form (conjugate) is essential. This chapter introduces the most fundamental verbs: the auxiliary verbs **essere** (to be) and **avere** (to have), and the pattern for conjugating regular verbs in the present indicative tense.

The Present Indicative is used to describe actions happening now, states of being, and general facts.

---

## Part 1: The Essential Auxiliary Verbs – Essere and Avere

Unlike English, where we have a single verb "to be," Italian uses two primary verbs for existence and possession: *essere* and *avere*. Both are crucial because they are used not only as main verbs but also as auxiliary verbs (helping verbs) to form compound tenses.

### 1. Essere (To Be)

*Essere* is used to describe identity, location, characteristics, and state of being.

#### Conjugation of *Essere* (Present Indicative)

| Pronoun | Italian Form | English Translation | Notes |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Io | **sono** | I am | |
| Tu | **sei** | You are (singular, informal) | |
| Lui / Lei | **è** | He / She / It is | |
| Noi | **siamo** | We are | |
| Voi | **siete** | You are (plural) | Used when addressing a group |
| Loro | **sono** | They are | |

**Key Rule: Agreement with *Essere***
When using *essere*, the adjective or the participle that follows must agree in gender and number with the subject.

*   *Io sono* **stanco** (I am tired).
*   *Noi siamo* **felici** (We are happy).

### 2. Avere (To Have)

*Avere* is used to express possession, age, and certain physical states.

#### Conjugation of *Avere* (Present Indicative)

| Pronoun | Italian Form | English Translation | Notes |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Io | **ho** | I have | |
| Tu | **hai** | You have (singular, informal) | |
| Lui / Lei | **ha** | He / She / It has | |
| Noi | **abbiamo** | We have | |
| Voi | **avete** | You have (plural) | Used when addressing a group |
| Loro | **hanno** | They have | |

**Key Rule: Agreement with *Avere***
When using *avere*, the verb form itself does not change based on the subject (e.g., *Io ho*, *Loro hanno*).

*   *Io ho* **venti** (I have twenty).
*   *Loro hanno* **cinque libri** (They have five books).

## Part 2: Conjugating Regular Verbs

Regular verbs are those that follow predictable patterns. They are categorized based on their infinitive endings: **-are**, **-ere**, and **-ire**.

### 1. Regular Verbs Ending in -ARE (e.g., Parlare – to speak)

The stem is found by removing the **-are**. The endings are added to this stem.

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: Parlare (to speak) | English Translation |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Io | **-o** | io parlo | I speak |
| Tu | **-i** | tu parli | You speak |
| Lui / Lei | **-a** | lui parla | He speaks |
| Noi | **-iamo** | noi parliamo | We speak |
| Voi | **-ate** | voi parlate | You speak (pl.) |
| Loro | **-ano** | loro parlano | They speak |

### 2. Regular Verbs Ending in -ERE (e.g., Credere – to believe)

The stem is found by removing the **-ere**. The endings are added to this stem.

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: Credere (to believe) | English Translation |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Io | **-o** | io credo | I believe |
| Tu | **-i** | tu credi | You believe |
| Lui / Lei | **-e** | lui crede | He believes |
| Noi | **-iamo** | noi crediamo | We believe |
| Voi | **-ete** | voi credete | You believe (pl.) |
| Loro | **-ono** | loro credono | They believe |

### 3. Regular Verbs Ending in -IRE (e.g., Dormire – to sleep)

The stem is found by removing the **-ire**. The endings are added to this stem.

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: Dormire (to sleep) | English Translation |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Io | **-o** | io dormo | I sleep |
| Tu | **-i** | tu dormi | You sleep |
| Lui / Lei | **-e** | lui dorme | He sleeps |
| Noi | **-iamo** | noi dormiamo | We sleep |
| Voi | **-ite** | voi dormite | You sleep (pl.) |
| Loro | **-ono** | loro dormono | They sleep |

## Part 3: Irregular Verbs (Introduction)

While the patterns above cover the majority of verbs, some common verbs are highly irregular and must be memorized. The most important irregular verbs in the present tense are *essere* and *avere* (which we have already mastered), but we must also note the irregular forms of high-frequency verbs.

**Example: Andare (to go) – An Irregular Verb**

| Pronoun | Andare (to go) | English Translation |

| Lui / Lei | **va** | He / She / It goes |
| Noi | **andiamo** | We go |

## Part 4: Application and Context

### Using *Essere* vs. *Avere* (The Crucial Distinction)

In English, we translate "to be" directly. In Italian, the choice between *essere* and *avere* is not just about the direct translation; it is about the verb required by the structure of the sentence.

1.  **Use *Essere***: For permanent states, identity, location, and descriptions (using adjectives).
    *   *Io **sono** italiano.* (I **am** Italian. – Identity)
    *   *Tu **sei** alto.* (You **are** tall. – Description)

2.  **Use *Avere***: For possession, age, and certain physical states (using nouns).
    *   *Io **ho** fame.* (I **have** hunger. – Possession/State)
    *   *Lui **ha** vent'anni.* (He **has** twenty years. – Age)

### Integrating Vocabulary (Numbers 20-100)

When using *avere* to express numbers, the number itself is the subject of the verb *avere*.

| Number | Italian | Example Sentence | English Translation |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 20 | venti | Io **ho** venti anni. | I have twenty years. |
| 35 | trentacinque | Noi **abbiamo** trentacinque libri. | We have thirty-five books. |
| 99 | novantanove | Loro **hanno** novantanove euro. | They have ninety-nine euros. |

## Part 5: Example Sentences

Here are eight examples demonstrating the use of *essere*, *avere*, and regular verb conjugations in context.

1.  **Io sono** stanco oggi.
    *   *(Essere, Adjective)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** I am tired today.
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** I am tired today.

2.  **Tu hai** fame?
    *   *(Avere)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** You have hunger?
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** Are you hungry? (Note: Hunger is often expressed using *avere* in this context.)

3.  **Noi siamo** studenti.
    *   *(Essere)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** We are students.
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** We are students.

4.  **Lui ha** trentacinque anni.
    *   *(Avere)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** He has thirty-five years.
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** He is thirty-five years old.

5.  **Voi parlate** italiano bene.
    *   *(Parlare - -are ending)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** You (pl.) speak Italian well.
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** You all speak Italian well.

6.  **Loro dormono** molto.
    *   *(Dormire - -ire ending)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** They sleep a lot.
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** They sleep a lot.

7.  **Io ho** dieci libri.
    *   *(Avere)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** I have ten books.
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** I have ten books.

8.  **Lei è** molto gentile.
    *   *(Essere)*
    *   **Literal Translation:** She is very kind.
    *   **Idiomatic Translation:** She is very kind.

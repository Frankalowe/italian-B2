## Introduction to the Vocabulary

Welcome to this essential vocabulary module. To master reflexive verbs like *svegliarsi* (to wake up) or *vestirsi* (to get dressed), you need the vocabulary that describes *what* you do and *how* you feel during those routines. This sheet provides the foundational words necessary to build complex sentences at the A2 level.

## Summary & Grammar Note for A2 Learners

**Key Takeaway:** When using reflexive verbs (like *lavarsi* or *vestirsi*), remember that the reflexive pronoun (*mi, ti, si, ci, vi*) must always precede the conjugated verb.

**Example Structure:**
*   **Io** (I) + **mi** (reflexive pronoun) + **lavo** (wash) $\rightarrow$ *Io mi lavo.* (I wash myself.)
*   **Tu** (You) + **ti** (reflexive pronoun) + **vesti** (dress) $\rightarrow$ *Tu ti vesti.* (You get dressed.)

**Practice Tip:** Try to create three sentences for each theme, ensuring you use at least one reflexive verb and one vocabulary word from that theme!

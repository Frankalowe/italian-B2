## Professor's Notes on Usage (Grammar Focus)

**Key Takeaway for A2 Learners:**

When you use the comparative structures ($\text{più/meno/di/che}$), you are comparing **two** things.

1.  **Use $\text{più}$ and $\text{meno}$** for simple, direct comparisons (e.g., *più grande* = bigger).
2.  **Use $\text{di}$ and $\text{che}$** when comparing attributes (e.g., *più alto **di**...* or *più grande **che**...*).
3.  **Use $\text{come}$** when expressing similarity (e.g., *è **come**...*).

**Irregular Forms:**
Memorize the irregular forms immediately. These are crucial for sounding natural:
*   **Ottimo** (Excellent) – Used for quality.
*   **Migliore** (Better) – The comparative of *buono*.
*   **Il migliore** (The best) – The superlative.

# Unit: Intro to Imperfect & Future Tenses

## Grammar Overview

In this unit, we will explore two important tenses in Italian: the **Imperfetto** (Imperfect) and the **Futuro Semplice** (Simple Future). These tenses are essential for describing past situations and making future predictions or plans. 

### 1. Imperfetto (Imperfect Tense)

#### 1.1 Usage

The Imperfetto is used to describe:
- Ongoing actions in the past.
- Habitual actions or routines in the past.
- Descriptions of people, places, and situations in the past.
- Background information in a narrative.

#### 1.2 Conjugation

The Imperfetto is formed by taking the stem of the verb and adding the appropriate endings. 

**Regular Verb Conjugations:**

| Verb Type  | Ending | Conjugation |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| **-are**   | -avo, -avi, -ava, -avamo, -avate, -avano | parlare: parlavo, parlavi, parlava, parlavamo, parlavate, parlavano |
| **-ere**   | -evo, -evi, -eva, -evamo, -evate, -evano | vedere: vedevo, vedevi, vedeva, vedevamo, vedevate, vedevano |
| **-ire**   | -ivo, -ivi, -iva, -ivamo, -ivate, -ivano | dormire: dormivo, dormivi, dormiva, dormivamo, dormivate, dormivano |

**Irregular Verbs:**

| Verb      | Conjugation |
|-----------|-------------|
| essere    | ero, eri, era, eravamo, eravate, erano |
| avere     | avevo, avevi, aveva, avevamo, avevate, avevano |
| fare      | facevo, facevi, faceva, facevamo, facevate, facevano |
| dire      | dicevo, dicevi, diceva, dicevamo, dicevate, dicevano |

#### 1.3 Examples

1. **Italian:** Quando ero bambino, giocavo a calcio.  
   **Literal Translation:** When I was a child, I played soccer.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** When I was a kid, I used to play soccer.

2. **Italian:** Ogni estate, andavamo al mare.  
   **Literal Translation:** Every summer, we went to the sea.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** Every summer, we used to go to the beach.

3. **Italian:** La casa era grande e bella.  
   **Literal Translation:** The house was big and beautiful.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** The house was spacious and lovely.

4. **Italian:** Mentre studiavo, ascoltavo la musica.  
   **Literal Translation:** While I was studying, I listened to music.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** While I was studying, I was listening to music.

### 2. Futuro Semplice (Simple Future Tense)

#### 2.1 Usage

The Futuro Semplice is used to express:
- Actions that will happen in the future.
- Predictions about the future.
- Intentions or plans.

#### 2.2 Conjugation

The Futuro Semplice is formed by taking the infinitive form of the verb and adding the appropriate endings. 

**Regular Verb Conjugations:**

| Verb Type  | Ending | Conjugation |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| **-are**   | -erò, -erai, -erà, -eremo, -erete, -eranno | parlare: parlerò, parlerai, parlerà, parleremo, parlerete, parleranno |
| **-ere**   | -erò, -erai, -erà, -eremo, -erete, -eranno | vedere: vedrò, vedrai, vedrà, vedremo, vedrete, vedranno |
| **-ire**   | -irò, -irai, -irà, -iremo, -irete, -iranno | dormire: dormirò, dormirai, dormirà, dormiremo, dormirete, dormiranno |

**Irregular Verbs:**

| Verb      | Conjugation |
|-----------|-------------|
| essere    | sarò, sarai, sarà, saremo, sarete, saranno |
| avere     | avrò, avrai, avrà, avremo, avrete, avranno |
| fare      | farò, farai, farà, faremo, farete, faranno |
| andare     | andrò, andrai, andrà, andremo, andrete, andranno |
| venire    | verrò, verrai, verrà, verremo, verrete, verranno |

#### 2.3 Examples

1. **Italian:** Domani andrò al cinema.  
   **Literal Translation:** Tomorrow I will go to the cinema.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** Tomorrow I will go to the movies.

2. **Italian:** L'anno prossimo, viaggeremo in Italia.  
   **Literal Translation:** Next year, we will travel to Italy.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** Next year, we will take a trip to Italy.

3. **Italian:** Credo che pioverà domani.  
   **Literal Translation:** I believe that it will rain tomorrow.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** I think it will rain tomorrow.

4. **Italian:** Farò i compiti più tardi.  
   **Literal Translation:** I will do the homework later.  
   **Idiomatic Translation:** I will do my homework later.

### Summary

Understanding the Imperfetto and Futuro Semplice tenses is crucial for effective communication in Italian. The Imperfetto allows you to describe past situations and habitual actions, while the Futuro Semplice helps you express future intentions and predictions. Practice conjugating both regular and irregular verbs in these tenses to build a solid foundation for your Italian language skills.

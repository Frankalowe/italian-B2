# Chapter: Relative Pronouns in Italian

## Introduction to Relative Pronouns

Relative pronouns are essential in Italian as they allow us to connect clauses and provide additional information about a noun without starting a new sentence. This chapter focuses on the following relative pronouns:

- **che** (that, which, who)
- **cui** (whom, whose, to whom, for whom)
- **chi** (he who, whoever)
- **quello che / ciò che** (what, that which)

## 1. **Subject and Object Relative Pronouns**

### 1.1. **Che**

**Usage**: "Che" is the most versatile relative pronoun and can refer to both subjects and objects in a sentence.

- **Subject**: Refers to the subject of the relative clause.
- **Object**: Refers to the object of the relative clause.

**Examples**:
1. La casa **che** ho comprato è bella.  
   *(The house **that** I bought is beautiful.)*
   
2. Il libro **che** leggo è interessante.  
   *(The book **that** I am reading is interesting.)*

### 1.2. **Cui**

**Usage**: "Cui" is used to refer to people or things and is always preceded by a preposition. It can denote possession, direction, or relation.

**Examples**:
1. La persona **cui** parli è mia sorella.  
   *(The person **to whom** you are speaking is my sister.)*
   
2. Il libro **cui** ho pensato è molto famoso.  
   *(The book **that** I thought of is very famous.)*

### 1.3. **Chi**

**Usage**: "Chi" is used to refer to people and translates as "he who" or "whoever." It is often used in general statements.

**Examples**:
1. **Chi** studia, ottiene buoni risultati.  
   *(**Whoever** studies gets good results.)*
   
2. **Chi** ama gli animali, è una persona gentile.  
   *(**He who** loves animals is a kind person.)*

### 1.4. **Quello che / Ciò che**

**Usage**: "Quello che" and "ciò che" are used to refer to things or ideas, translating to "that which" or "what." They are often used when the antecedent is not explicitly stated.

**Examples**:
1. **Quello che** hai detto è molto interessante.  
   *(**What** you said is very interesting.)*
   
2. **Ciò che** voglio è la verità.  
   *(**What** I want is the truth.)*

## 2. **Grammatical Breakdown**

### 2.1. **Structure of Relative Clauses**

A relative clause typically follows the noun it modifies. The structure is as follows:

- **Main Clause** + **Relative Pronoun** + **Relative Clause**

**Example**:
- Ho visto un film **che** mi è piaciuto.  
  *(I saw a movie **that** I liked.)*

### 2.2. **Prepositions with Cui**

When using "cui," the preposition must agree with the context of the sentence. Common prepositions include:

- di (of)
- a (to)
- per (for)
- con (with)
- da (from)

**Examples**:
1. Il ragazzo **di cui** ti ho parlato è qui.  
   *(The boy **of whom** I told you is here.)*
   
2. La casa **a cui** penso è molto grande.  
   *(The house **to which** I am thinking is very big.)*

## 3. **Pronoun Forms**

### 3.1. **Relative Pronouns Overview**

| Pronoun         | English Equivalent | Usage                          |

| che              | that, which, who  | Subject or object              |

| quello che       | that which        | Refers to things or ideas      |
| ciò che          | what              | Refers to things or ideas      |

## 5. **Example Sentences**

1. La macchina **che** ho comprato è rossa.  
   *(The car **that** I bought is red.)*

2. La ragazza **cui** ho dato il libro è mia amica.  
   *(The girl **to whom** I gave the book is my friend.)*

3. **Chi** lavora sodo, avrà successo.  
   *(**He who** works hard will succeed.)*

4. **Quello che** desidero è un po' di pace.  
   *(**What** I desire is a bit of peace.)*

5. Ho visto il film **che** mi hai consigliato.  
   *(I saw the movie **that** you recommended.)*

6. La casa **cui** stai pensando è in vendita.  
   *(The house **that** you are thinking of is for sale.)*

7. **Chi** ha bisogno di aiuto, può chiedere.  
   *(**Whoever** needs help can ask.)*

8. **Ciò che** conta è la tua felicità.  
   *(**What** matters is your happiness.)*

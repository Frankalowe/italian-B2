# Unit: Imperfetto & Past Contrasts

## Grammar Guide

### 1. Overview of Imperfetto

The **Imperfetto** is a past tense used to describe ongoing actions, habitual actions, background states, and descriptions in the past. It contrasts with the **Passato Prossimo**, which is used for completed actions.

#### Key Uses of Imperfetto:
- **Ongoing Actions**: Actions that were in progress in the past.
- **Habitual Actions**: Actions that were repeated or habitual in the past.
- **Descriptions**: Background information, such as weather, time, age, and physical or emotional states.

### 2. Conjugation of Imperfetto

#### Regular Verbs

The Imperfetto is formed by taking the stem of the verb and adding the appropriate endings. 

**Conjugation Endings**:
- **-are verbs**: -avo, -avi, -ava, -avamo, -avate, -avano
- **-ere verbs**: -evo, -evi, -eva, -evamo, -evate, -evano
- **-ire verbs**: -ivo, -ivi, -iva, -ivamo, -ivate, -ivano

**Example Conjugations**:

| Verb Type | Infinitive | io      | tu      | lui/lei | noi      | voi      | loro     |
|-----------|------------|---------|---------|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| -are      | parlare    | parlavo | parlavi | parlava | parlavamo| parlavate| parlavano|
| -ere      | credere    | credevo | credevi | credeva | credevamo| credevate| credevano|
| -ire      | dormire    | dormivo | dormivi | dormiva | dormivamo| dormivate| dormivano|

#### Irregular Verbs

Some verbs have irregular forms in the Imperfetto. The most notable is **essere**.

| Verb     | io      | tu      | lui/lei | noi      | voi      | loro     |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| essere   | ero     | eri     | era     | eravamo  | eravate  | erano    |
| bere     | bevevo  | bevevi  | beveva  | bevevamo | bevevate | bevevano |
| fare     | facevo  | facevi  | faceva  | facevamo | facevate | facevano |

### 3. Choosing Between Imperfetto and Passato Prossimo

When narrating past events, it is crucial to know when to use the Imperfetto versus the Passato Prossimo. 

#### Key Differences:
- **Passato Prossimo**: Used for actions that have been completed and have a clear beginning and end.
- **Imperfetto**: Used for actions that were ongoing, habitual, or provided background information.

#### Examples:
1. **Passato Prossimo**: 
   - **Italian**: Ieri ho mangiato una pizza. 
   - **Literal Translation**: Yesterday I ate a pizza.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: I had a pizza yesterday.

2. **Imperfetto**: 
   - **Italian**: Quando ero bambino, mangiavo sempre la pizza.
   - **Literal Translation**: When I was a child, I always ate pizza.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: When I was a kid, I used to eat pizza all the time.

### 5. Example Sentences

Here are varied example sentences that illustrate the use of Imperfetto and Passato Prossimo:

1. **Imperfetto**: 
   - **Italian**: Ogni estate andavamo al mare.
   - **Literal Translation**: Every summer we went to the sea.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: Every summer, we used to go to the beach.

2. **Passato Prossimo**: 
   - **Italian**: L'anno scorso ho studiato all'estero.
   - **Literal Translation**: Last year I studied abroad.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: Last year, I studied abroad.

3. **Imperfetto**: 
   - **Italian**: Quando ero all'università, leggevo molti libri.
   - **Literal Translation**: When I was at university, I read many books.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: When I was in college, I used to read a lot of books.

4. **Passato Prossimo**: 
   - **Italian**: Ieri ho fatto un esame difficile.
   - **Literal Translation**: Yesterday I took a difficult exam.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: Yesterday, I had a tough exam.

5. **Imperfetto**: 
   - **Italian**: Da bambino, giocavo sempre con i miei amici.
   - **Literal Translation**: As a child, I always played with my friends.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: As a kid, I used to play with my friends all the time.

6. **Passato Prossimo**: 
   - **Italian**: Ho ricevuto un bel regalo per il mio compleanno.
   - **Literal Translation**: I received a nice gift for my birthday.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: I got a lovely gift for my birthday.

7. **Imperfetto**: 
   - **Italian**: Durante le vacanze, faceva sempre caldo.
   - **Literal Translation**: During the holidays, it was always hot.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: It was always hot during the holidays.

8. **Passato Prossimo**: 
   - **Italian**: Ho visto un film interessante la settimana scorsa.
   - **Literal Translation**: I saw an interesting film last week.
   - **Idiomatic Translation**: I watched an interesting movie last week.

### Conclusion

Understanding the distinctions between the Imperfetto and Passato Prossimo is crucial for narrating past events in Italian. By mastering these concepts and practicing with relevant vocabulary, learners can effectively communicate their experiences and memories.
